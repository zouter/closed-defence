What causes disease? How can we improve crop yield? How do (our) cells functions? What is inside (our) cells?
1 min Ever noticed how the biggest questions in biology, such as finding the cause of a disease or trying to improve crop yield, are boiled down to understanding the tinniest. I find this a bit funny too, to solve the biggest, we need to understand the tinniest. That's why I'm really happy that during my PhD I was able to work on solving perhaps very tiny questions, such as finding the right tool to analyse some dataset, but which can allow us to go back to these "bigger" questions and try to address them.
