Big questions often have tiny answers
1 min It's a bit ironic that the big questions that we want to answer, such as causes of disease or how to make crops grow better, ultimately lead us to smaller questions about cells and the molecules therein. In my PhD I studied one particular kind of molecule, mRNAs. That's why I'm really happy that during my PhD I was able to work on solving perhaps very tiny questions, such as finding the right tool to analyse some dataset, but which can allow us to go back to these "bigger" questions and try to address them.

My goals:

  • Fill the gaps where possible
  • Compare methods where needed
My goals were pragmatic here: I wanted to fill the gaps whem I came across them, and wherever it was needed I also wanted to compare different methods.
